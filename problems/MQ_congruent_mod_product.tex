\begin{problem}
Prove that if $a \equiv b \pmod{14}$ and $a \equiv b \pmod{5}$, then
$a \equiv b \pmod {70}$.

\begin{solution}
We know $a \equiv b \pmod{14}$ means $14 \divides a - b$.  Likewise,
$a \equiv b \pmod{5}$ means $5 \divides a - b$.  Also, 14 and 5 are
relatively prime.

But if $m,n$, are relatively prime and $m$ and $n$ both divide $x$,
then $mn \divides x$.  So, applying that reasoning with $x = a - b$,
$m = 14$ and $n = 5$ yields $70 \divides a - b$, proving $a \equiv b
\pmod {70}$ as required.

This also follows straightforwardly from the Chinese Remainder Theorem,
described in Problem~\bref{CP_chinese_remainder}.
\end{solution}

\end{problem}
