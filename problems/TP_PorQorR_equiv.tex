\begin{problem}
Prove that the propositional formulas
\[
P \QOR Q \QOR R
\]
and
\[
(P \QAND \QNOT(Q)) \QOR (Q \QAND \QNOT(R)) \QOR (R \QAND \QNOT(P)) \QOR (P \QAND Q \QAND R).
\]are equivalent.

\begin{solution}
  We compare $(P \QOR Q \QOR R)$ and $K \eqdef (P \QAND \QNOT(Q)) \QOR (Q \QAND
  \QNOT(R)) \QOR (R \QAND \QNOT(P)) \QOR (P \QAND Q \QAND R)$ using a truth
  table:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{ c c c | c | c c c c | c }
  $P$ & $Q$ & $R$ & $P  \QOR  Q  \QOR  R$ & $P  \QAND  \bar{Q}$ & $Q  \QAND \bar{R}$ &  $R  \QAND  \bar{P}$ & $P  \QAND  Q  \QAND  R$ & $K$\\
  \hline
  T & T & T & \textbf{T} & F & F & F & T & \textbf{T} \\
  T & T & F & \textbf{T} & F & T & F & F & \textbf{T} \\
  T & F & T & \textbf{T} & T & F & F & F & \textbf{T} \\
  T & F & F & \textbf{T} & T & F & F & F & \textbf{T} \\
  F & T & T & \textbf{T} & F & F & T & F & \textbf{T} \\
  F & T & F & \textbf{T} & F & T & F & F & \textbf{T} \\
  F & F & T & \textbf{T} & F & F & T & F & \textbf{T} \\
  F & F & F & \textbf{F} & F & F & F & F & \textbf{F} \\
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Both $(P \QOR Q \QOR R)$ and $K$ have identical truth tables, thus the two
statements are equivalent.

\end{solution}
\end{problem}
