\begin{problem}
Describe an example of two \textbf{un}countable sets $A$ and $B$ such
that there is no bijection between $A$ and $B$.

\begin{solution}
Let $A$ be any uncountable set such as the real numbers, the infinite
binary strings, or the powerset of integers, and let $B$ be the power
set of $A$.
\end{solution}

\end{problem}
