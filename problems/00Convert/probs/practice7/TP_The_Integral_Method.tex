\begin{problem}

%% type: short-answer
%% title: The Integral Method

Let
\begin{equation*}
S ::= \sum_{n=1}^5 n^{\frac{1}{3}}
\end{equation*}
Using the \textbf{Integral Method}, we can find integers, $a$, $b$,
$c$, $d$, and a real number, $e$, such that
\begin{equation*}
    \int_a^b x^e \,dx \le S \le \int_c^d x^e \,dx
\end{equation*}

What are appropriate values for $a$--$e$ below?  

% Enter your answer as
% a parenthesized sequence 5 numbers, for example, (1 2 3 4 1/5).

\begin{solution}
0

5

1

6

1/3
\end{solution}
