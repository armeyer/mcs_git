\ppart
What is the value of $\phi(572)$, where $\phi$ is Euler's function?

\begin{solution}
$572 = 2^{2} \cdot 11 \cdot 13$, so $\phi(572)=(2^{2} - 2^{1})(11 - 1)(13 - 1) = 2 \cdot 10 \cdot 12 = 240$.
\end{solution}

\examspace[1.0in]

\ppart
What is the remainder of $95^{1200^5+1}$ divided by 572?

\begin{solution}
\begin{align*}
95^{1200^5+1} &= 95^{1200 \cdot 1200^{4}+1} \\
	 & = 95^{240 \cdot 5 \cdot 1200^{4}+1} \\
           & = (95^{240})^{5 \cdot 1200^4}  \cdot  95 \\
           & \equiv  1^{5 \cdot 1200^4}  \cdot  95 \bmod 572\\
           & \equiv  95 \bmod 572.
\end{align*}
\end{solution}

\examspace[2.0in]

\ppart Find the inverse of 95 modulo 572 in the interval
$\Zintvcc{1}{571}$?

\begin{solution}
We first use the Pulverizer to find $s,t$ such that $\gcd(95, 572) = s\cdot 95 + t\cdot 572$:
\[
\begin{array}{ccccrcl}
x & \quad & y & \quad & \rem{x}{y} & = & x - q \cdot y \\ \hline
572 && 95 && 2  & = &   572 - 6\cdot 95 \\
95 && 2 && 1 & = &   95 - 47\cdot 2 \\
&&&&            & = &   95 - 47\cdot (572 - 6\cdot 95)\\
&&&&            & = &  283\cdot 95 - 47\cdot 572\\
\end{array}
\]

\[
1 = 283 \cdot 95 - 47 \cdot 572.
\]
This implies that $s = 283$ is an inverse of 95 modulo 572.
\end{solution}

\ppart
What is the remainder of $22^{11999}$ divided by 175?

\begin{solution}

\begin{align*}
22^{11999} & \equiv 22^{11999} \cdot 22 \cdot 8 \bmod 175 \\
           & \equiv 22^{12000} \cdot 8 \bmod 175 \\
           &= 22^{(120 \cdot 100)} \cdot 8 \bmod 175 \\
           & = (22^{120})^{100} \cdot 8 \bmod 175 \\
           & \equiv  1^{100} \cdot 8 \bmod 175\\
           & \equiv  8 \bmod 175.
\end{align*}
\end{solution}
