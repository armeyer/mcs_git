\begin{problem}

%% type: short-answer
%% title: Biased Gambler's Ruin

Suppose that a gambler wins each \$1 bet with probability~0.49, and
the gambler keeps betting until he runs out of money or he reaches
some fixed goal of $T$ dollars.

Let $t(n)$ be the expected number of \emph{bets} the gambler makes
until the game ends, where $n$ is the number of dollars the gambler
has when he starts betting.  Then the function $t$ satisfies a linear
recurrence of the form
\begin{equation*}
t(n) = a \cdot t(n+1) + b \cdot t(n-1) + c
\end{equation*}
for real constants $a$, $b$, $c$ and $0 < n < T$.

\bparts

\ppart
What is the value of $a$?

\begin{solution}
0.49
\end{solution}

\ppart
What is the value of $b$?

\begin{solution}
0.51
\end{solution}

\ppart
What is the value of $c$?

\begin{solution}
1
\end{solution}

\ppart
What is $t(0)$?

\begin{solution}
0
\end{solution}

\ppart
What is $t(T)$?

\begin{solution}
0
\end{solution}

\eparts

\end{problem}
